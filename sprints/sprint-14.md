## Opening Scenario

Harold, 82, has had the same mobile plan for six years. He pays $85/month for 2 GB of data and unlimited talk and text. He never uses more than 500 MB because he only uses his phone for calls and the occasional photo text to his grandchildren. He has no idea he is overpaying by at least $40/month. His provider has never called to suggest a cheaper plan.

Start from this moment. Then teach.

## Key Data Points

- CRTC reports cellular prices declined 16.7% from 2023 to 2024
- Many carriers now offer $30–45/month plans with 4–6 GB for light users
- Data-only plans exist for iPad/tablet users who don't need voice
- Seniors who only call and text may benefit from prepaid plans ($15–25/month)
- Comparison tools: PlanHub.ca, WhistleOut Canada

## Sections to Build

1. **Harold's $40 Mistake** — how long-term customers get left on old plans, why providers don't volunteer savings
2. **What Do You Actually Use Your Phone For?** — self-assessment checklist: calls only, calls + texts, calls + texts + photos, calls + texts + browsing + streaming. Each maps to a plan tier.
3. **Understanding Mobile Data** — what 1 GB looks like in real usage. Visual: jar filling up. Email = drops, photos = cups, video = buckets.
4. **Reading Your Mobile Bill** — annotated example. Device payment vs plan cost vs extras vs taxes. Where the waste hides.
5. **Prepaid vs Postpaid** — plain language comparison. Prepaid: pay first, no surprises. Postpaid: pay after, possible overages. Which is right for you.
6. **How to Compare Plans** — same PlanHub/WhistleOut walkthrough as Module 20 but filtered for mobile.
7. **The Negotiation Call** — adapted phone script for mobile. Include: "I'd like to do a plan review. What's the best plan for someone who uses less than [X] GB per month?"
8. **Avoiding the Upgrade Trap** — why your phone works fine for another 2–3 years. When you actually need a new one. Buying unlocked vs locked.
9. **Quiz** — 5 questions
10. **What You Learned** — summary bullets
