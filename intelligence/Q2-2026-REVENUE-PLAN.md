## Risk Factors

1. **Sales cycle length** — SME decision-makers are slow. First audit may not close until late April or May.
2. **No existing pipeline** — Starting from zero. Every lead must be generated from scratch.
3. **Brand awareness** — Two Birds Innovation has no market presence yet. LinkedIn and outreach are the only channels.
4. **VW/PowerCo timeline** — The battery plant opportunity is real but may not translate to consulting revenue until 2027.
5. **Grant dependency** — Grants are slow and competitive. Cannot be a primary revenue strategy.
6. **Solo founder risk** — Aaron is the only revenue generator, delivery person, and business developer simultaneously.

---

## What Has to Go Right

1. **At least one audit closes in April.** This is the minimum viable outcome for the month. Without it, the plan slips by 30 days.
2. **Audit-to-retainer conversion works.** The entire revenue model depends on audit clients becoming retainer clients. If audits stay one-time, revenue caps at $2,500/month per client.
3. **LinkedIn builds credibility.** The 8-post calendar needs to generate inbound interest or at minimum make outbound warmer.
4. **Library outreach generates one warm conversation.** The DCC B2B channel is slow but high-value. One positive response justifies continued investment.
5. **Clarity drives inbound leads.** The free diagnostic tool needs to convert at least 1-2 users into consultation requests per month.

## Fallback Scenarios

**If April produces $0:**
- Extend outreach to 10 more contacts (credit unions, community orgs)
- Consider reducing audit price to $1,500 for first client (loss leader)
- Explore contract PM work on Upwork or Toptal as bridge income

**If retainer conversion fails:**
- Offer a smaller retainer package ($2,000/month, 2 days/month) as a lower commitment entry
- Package audit findings as a "done-for-you" implementation add-on ($1,500)
