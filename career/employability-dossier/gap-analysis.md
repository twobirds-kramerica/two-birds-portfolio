## 3. What Is NOT a Gap (Common Misconceptions)

### "You need to know how to code"
Wrong for evaluation roles. Aaron's non-developer perspective IS the value. He evaluates AI tools the way a real user would, not the way an engineer would. Companies need both. Most have too many engineers and not enough critical users.

### "You need a CS degree"
No evaluation role at Scale AI, Outlier, or similar platforms requires a CS degree. They require domain expertise (which Aaron has in product management) and the ability to assess AI output quality (which he demonstrates daily).

### "You need prior AI experience"
Aaron has 90 days of intensive, documented AI experience across 3 platforms (Claude, GPT, Gemini), 7 shipped products, and a sovereignty framework that most AI companies haven't built for their own operations. The experience exists. It's just not on a traditional CV yet.

### "You need to have worked at an AI company"
The AI evaluation industry specifically recruits domain experts from non-AI backgrounds. A 20-year PM who can spot a bad product recommendation is more valuable than an ML engineer who can't.

### "Your age is a disadvantage"
Aaron is 49. For evaluation roles, maturity and domain breadth are advantages. He's evaluated products across telecom, retail, ecommerce, and non-profit. A 25-year-old can't claim that.
