## The Reframe

Procrastination is not a moral failure — it's a listening failure. When someone procrastinates, their brain is sending a signal about the task. The job is to decode that signal, not override it with willpower.

Traditional advice fails because it treats the symptom (not doing the task) rather than the cause (why the brain is resisting). "Just start" and "break it into smaller steps" put people in the worst psychological state to actually move forward — frustrated and at war with themselves.

## The Two Arrows (Buddhist Framework)

**First Arrow:** The procrastination itself (scrolling instead of working, etc.)
- This isn't the real problem

**Second Arrow:** The emotional reaction to procrastinating (shame, guilt, self-judgment)
- This is what actually paralyzes people

Before any diagnostic work, help remove the second arrow. The goal is examining procrastination with kindness and curiosity, not judgment.

## The Diagnostic: Head, Hand, Heart

When procrastination is identified, the resistance comes from one of three places:

---

### HEAD — "Is this task appropriate?"

**The question:** Does this actually make strategic sense?

**Signs:**
- "I don't think this is going to work"
- "There's probably a better way"
- "This feels like busywork"
- Skepticism about whether it will achieve the goal

**Coaching questions:**
- "On a scale of 1–10, how confident are you this is the right approach?"
- "If you were advising someone else, would you tell them to do this?"
- "Is there a disconnect between what needs to be done and what you think should be done?"

**Solutions:**
- For small tasks with no dependencies: permission to remove it entirely
- For larger tasks: clarify strategic concerns, develop alternative proposals, practice the conversation with stakeholders

---

### HEART — "Is this task exciting?"

**The question:** What emotional resistance is present?

**Signs:**
- "I just dread opening this file"
- "Every time I think about it, I feel anxious"
- Irritation, resentment, or fear around the task
- Vague "I don't know why I can't do this"

**Coaching questions:**
- "When you picture doing this, what's the first feeling that comes up?"
- "Is there a person or situation connected to this that triggers something?"
- "If this task could talk, what would it be saying to you?"

**Solutions:**
- **Effective labeling:** Naming the specific emotion ("I'm afraid of being judged" vs. "I feel bad about this") is often enough
- **Pairing method:** If boring → pair with enjoyable activity (location, music, co-working). If daunting → pair with confidence boost (start with easiest part, work alongside someone)
- **Reframe purpose:** Connect to larger meaning, focus on who benefits, emphasize the feeling of closure
- **Address past patterns:** If linked to a previous bad experience, identify what's different this time

---

### HAND — "Is this task doable?"

**The question:** Is there a genuine capability gap?

**Signs:**
- "I've never done anything like this"
- "I don't know where to start"
- "What if I do it wrong?"
- Missing specific skills, knowledge, or tools

**Critical distinction:** Use this test — "If you absolutely had to do this right now, could you figure out the first step?" If yes, it's probably a Heart issue (confidence), not Hand (capability).

**Coaching questions:**
- "What specific skill or knowledge do you think you're missing?"
- "Is this actually a skills gap, or a confidence gap?"
- "Who could you ask for 30 minutes of guidance?"

**Solutions:**
- Get input from someone who's done it (usually best first step)
- Focused, time-limited learning (2 hours max, then apply)
- Identify minimum viable knowledge to start, not mastery

## Coaching Phrases

**For reframing:**
- "Your brain isn't broken — it's actually being smart. It's trying to protect you from something. Let's figure out what."
- "The problem isn't that you're procrastinating. The problem is how you're feeling about procrastinating."

**For diagnostic:**
- "Let's get curious rather than critical."
- "What if this resistance is trying to tell you something important?"

**For Heart issues:**
- "The stronger the feeling, the more important it is to lean in with curiosity, not judgment."
- "What's the story you're telling yourself about this task?"

**For solutions:**
- "What's the smallest version of this that doesn't trigger the resistance?"
- "Who could you borrow confidence from?"

## Integration with ADHD Coaching

When procrastination is identified, **do NOT default to standard ADHD tactics** (Pomodoro, breaking tasks down, 2-minute rule) until AFTER the diagnostic. Those tactics assume the problem is execution. Often the problem is:

- The task shouldn't be done at all (Head)
- There's an emotional block that needs addressing first (Heart)
- There's a genuine capability gap (Hand)

Applying productivity tactics to a Heart problem makes it worse — it adds shame ("I have all these tools and I STILL can't do it").

**Sequence: Diagnose → Address root cause → THEN apply execution tactics if needed.**

---

## When to Flag for Deeper Support

This protocol addresses normal procrastination. Flag if:
- Procrastination is consistent across ALL tasks (not task-specific)
- Strong shame/anxiety that doesn't decrease with reframing
- Signs of depression or other mental health concerns
- Procrastination tied to trauma

---

## The Meta-Insight

Sometimes procrastination reveals that the task shouldn't be done, or should be done differently, or points toward what someone actually wants to be doing instead. The goal isn't always to complete the avoided task — sometimes it's to learn what the avoidance is teaching.
