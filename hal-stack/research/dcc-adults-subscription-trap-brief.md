## The Idea (Aaron's words, reconstructed)

Two subscription traps Aaron wants seniors to understand:

### Trap 1: Free trial auto-renewals (short-term — 14 days)
- Services like Netflix, Disney+, Hulu, and many apps offer 14-day free trials or heavy discounts (e.g. 75% off)
- Common pattern: requires credit card upfront, auto-charges at full rate when trial ends
- Seniors often forget the trial end date and are charged full price without realising

**The move:** Subscribe AND cancel immediately. You keep the full benefit of the offer until the promotional period ends (you've paid for it, cancellation just prevents the auto-renewal). AND: cancelling immediately puts you on their "win-back" list — you'll receive strong retention/win-back offers to come back, often at better rates than the original promotion.

### Trap 2: ISP and cell phone promotional rates (long-term — 1-2 years)
- Internet providers and cell carriers often lock in a promotional rate for 12-24 months
- Easy to forget the rate increase that kicks in after the promo period
- The price jump can be significant ($20-50/month more) — the first bill after the rate change is often the only notification

**The move:** When you sign up for any internet or phone plan with a promotional period, immediately set a digital calendar reminder for 2 weeks BEFORE the promo ends — that gives you time to negotiate, switch, or cancel before the higher rate hits.

## Content Structure (proposed)

### Section 1: The streaming trial trap
- How free trials work (the business model: they want your card so the charge is automatic)
- The "subscribe and cancel immediately" method — walk through exactly how to cancel on Netflix, Amazon, Disney+, Apple TV+
- What "cancel" actually means (you don't lose access immediately — you keep it until the paid period ends)
- The win-back offer angle: cancelling puts you in their re-engagement funnel; expect a better offer within 30-60 days

### Section 2: The internet and phone plan time bomb
- How promotional pricing works (locked rate for 12-24 months, then full rate)
- The "set a calendar reminder now" method — walk through setting a reminder on iPad/iPhone/Android
- What to do when the reminder fires: call the provider, say "my promotional rate is ending," ask what retention offers they have. Providers almost always have something.

### Section 3: The general principle — subscriptions need a caretaker
- Digital subscriptions are silent: they never remind you they exist until the charge appears
- One practical system: check your credit card statement for subscriptions monthly
- The "Settings → subscriptions" check on iPhone/iPad (already in Module 4 wizard — cross-link)
