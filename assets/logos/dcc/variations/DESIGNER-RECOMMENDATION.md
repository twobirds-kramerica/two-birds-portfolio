# DCC Logo — Designer Recommendation

## Top 3 Ranked

### #1 Recommendation: DCC-V07 — Heart + Light Bulb

**Why:** This is the variation that most honestly represents what DCC does — it helps people understand technology, and it does so with genuine care. The light bulb is universally understood as "understanding" or "idea." The heart inside it says "we care about your journey, not just your skills." The silhouette is memorable and distinct from any tech company logo. No one would confuse this with a Silicon Valley startup.

**Favicon:** The bulb silhouette works at small sizes. The heart becomes a subtle hint — you feel it even if you can't see it at 16px.

**72-year-old in Ontario:** This is the logo a grandmother would point to and say "that's the one with the heart." It's approachable, non-threatening, and suggests that someone cares whether she succeeds.

**Best communicates "you are safe here":** The combination of warmth (heart) and illumination (bulb) says "we'll help you understand, and we'll be kind about it."

---

### #2 Recommendation: DCC-V01 — Shield + Checkmark

**Why:** The strongest "trust" signal in the set. For an audience that is anxious about technology — especially scams, data theft, and making mistakes — a shield with a checkmark says "you're protected." This is the logo that would appear on a certificate of completion and make seniors feel they've accomplished something real.

**Favicon:** Excellent. The simplest high-quality favicon in the set. Recognisable at every size.

**72-year-old in Ontario:** This feels credible and institutional (in a good way). A library director would feel comfortable putting this logo next to their own.

**Best communicates "you are safe here":** The shield IS safety. The checkmark IS confidence. Together they're the most literal interpretation of "digital confidence."

---

### #3 Recommendation: DCC-V08 — Open Door

**Why:** The most inviting logo. For someone who has been avoiding technology — who feels left behind, embarrassed, or afraid — an open door with warm light says "come in, it's okay, we're waiting for you." This is the logo that lowers the barrier to entry.

**Favicon:** Moderate. The door frame reads at 16px but loses the light ray detail.

**72-year-old in Ontario:** This feels like a community centre, not a tech company. "I'll just go have a look" energy.

**Best communicates "you are safe here":** The warmest, most physically inviting logo. But it communicates "welcome" more than "confidence." For the landing page, that might be exactly right.

---

## Which One as Favicon for the .io Site?

**DCC-V01 (Shield + Checkmark)** for the favicon specifically. It's the most legible at 16px and 32px, and "verified/trusted" is the right message for a browser tab.

**DCC-V07 (Heart + Light Bulb)** for the full site logo. It tells the warmer, more complete brand story when there's room for it.

Consider using both: V01 as the favicon, V07 as the header/about-page logo.

## Which Variations to Skip

- **DCC-V04** (arrow): Too corporate. Looks like an upload button or a fitness app. Doesn't feel like a learning community.
- **DCC-V06** (hands): Beautiful concept but too complex for a logo. Save it for illustration or marketing graphics, not the brand mark.
- **DCC-V02** (sunrise): Nice but generic. Many organisations use sunrise imagery. Not distinctive enough for DCC.

## Final Word

V07 (heart-bulb) is the recommendation because it captures what makes DCC genuinely different from other digital literacy programmes: it leads with empathy, not expertise. The heart says "we care." The bulb says "you'll understand." Together they say "Digital Confidence Centre" without a single word.
