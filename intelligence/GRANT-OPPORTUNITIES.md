# Grant Opportunities — Two Birds Innovation

**Last Updated:** April 1, 2026
**Purpose:** Track grant and funding opportunities relevant to Two Birds Innovation and the Digital Confidence Centre.

⚠️ **Important:** All deadlines and program statuses must be verified before acting. Government programs change frequently. Check the source URL before submitting anything.

## CDAP — Canada Digital Adoption Program
- **Administering body:** Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada (ISED)
- **What it funds:** Digital adoption plans and technology implementation for SMEs
- **Amount:** Up to $15,000 grant + $100,000 interest-free loan through BDC
- **Eligibility:** Canadian-owned SME, 1+ employee, $500K–$100M revenue
- **Application complexity:** Medium
- **Fit score for Two Birds:** 4/5 — Aaron could be a registered CDAP digital advisor, AND Two Birds clients could access the grant
- **Current status:** Program was paused in 2024. Check https://ised-isde.canada.ca/site/canada-digital-adoption-program/ for reopening
- **Deadline:** Rolling when open
- **Two Birds angle:** Register as a CDAP advisor. Clients get subsidised consulting; Two Birds gets paid through the program.

## New Horizons for Seniors Program (NHSP)
- **Administering body:** Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC)
- **What it funds:** Community projects that improve quality of life for seniors
- **Amount:** Up to $25,000 per project (community-based stream)
- **Eligibility:** Not-for-profit organisations, municipalities, Indigenous organisations. Individual businesses not eligible — would need a partner org (library, seniors' centre).
- **Application complexity:** Medium
- **Fit score for Two Birds:** 3/5 — requires partnership with eligible org, but DCC is a strong fit for the program intent
- **Current status:** Annual intake, typically opens in spring/summer
- **Deadline:** Check https://www.canada.ca/en/employment-social-development/programs/new-horizons-seniors.html
- **Two Birds angle:** Partner with a local library or seniors' centre. They apply; DCC is the program deliverable.

---

## Ontario Seniors Community Grant Program (OSCGP)
- **Administering body:** Ontario Ministry for Seniors and Accessibility
- **What it funds:** Programs and activities that help seniors stay connected, active, and engaged
- **Amount:** Up to $25,000 per project
- **Eligibility:** Incorporated not-for-profit organisations in Ontario
- **Application complexity:** Low–Medium
- **Fit score for Two Birds:** 3/5 — same partnership requirement as NHSP
- **Current status:** Annual program. Check Ontario.ca for current intake status
- **Deadline:** Typically spring intake
- **Two Birds angle:** Same as NHSP — partner with eligible org, position DCC as the digital literacy component.

## SBEC — Small Business Enterprise Centre (St. Thomas / Elgin County)
- **Administering body:** City of St. Thomas Economic Development
- **What it funds:** Small business support, training, mentorship, starter grants
- **Amount:** Varies — some programs offer $5,000–$10,000 micro-grants
- **Eligibility:** Businesses located in St. Thomas / Elgin County
- **Application complexity:** Low
- **Fit score for Two Birds:** 5/5 — local, directly relevant, Aaron qualifies
- **Current status:** Active. Contact SBEC directly.
- **Deadline:** Rolling / program-dependent
- **Two Birds angle:** Apply for any available micro-grants. Also explore whether Two Birds can be listed as a resource provider for SBEC's digital literacy referrals.
- **Contact:** https://www.stthomas.ca/business-development/small-business-enterprise-centre

---

## Ontario Trillium Foundation (OTF)
- **Administering body:** Ontario Trillium Foundation (arm's-length agency of Ontario government)
- **What it funds:** Community grants across Ontario — Seed, Grow, and Resilient Communities streams
- **Amount:** Seed: up to $75,000 / Grow: up to $150,000
- **Eligibility:** Not-for-profit organisations, Indigenous communities, municipalities. Not individual businesses.
- **Application complexity:** High
- **Fit score for Two Birds:** 2/5 — requires not-for-profit partner and significant application effort
- **Current status:** Active, continuous intake
- **Deadline:** Check https://www.otf.ca/
- **Two Birds angle:** Long shot unless partnered with a registered not-for-profit. Could work if a library system or seniors' organisation wanted to scale DCC regionally.

---

## Heritage Canada — Digital Literacy Grants
- **Administering body:** Canadian Heritage
- **What it funds:** Digital citizenship, media literacy, combating disinformation
- **Amount:** Varies by program
- **Eligibility:** Not-for-profit organisations, educational institutions
- **Application complexity:** High
- **Fit score for Two Birds:** 2/5 — same not-for-profit requirement
- **Current status:** Check https://www.canada.ca/en/canadian-heritage.html
- **Two Birds angle:** Only viable with institutional partner. DCC's bilingual content and scam protection modules are strong fit for program intent.
