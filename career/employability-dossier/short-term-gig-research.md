# Short-Term AI Gig Platform Research

Researched April 12, 2026. Sources: platform websites, web fetches. Pay rates are approximate and change frequently.

---

## 1. Outlier AI (outlier.ai) -- operated by Scale AI

**What it is:** Expert platform for providing human feedback to improve LLMs. Tasks include writing prompts, creating grading rubrics, rating/ranking AI answers.
**Canadian eligibility:** Not explicitly confirmed. Operates in 50+ countries. Likely available but needs verification during signup.
**Application:** Create profile, import skills, verify identity, verify skills. 30-90 minute onboarding.
**Pay:** Not disclosed per-task. Collective $500M+ paid. Trustpilot rating 4.1/5 from 3,000+ reviews. Industry reports suggest US$15-40/hour depending on expertise domain.
**Work volume:** Variable. Some reviewers report inconsistent task availability. Others report steady work.
**Time to first paid task:** 1-2 weeks (onboarding + identity verification + skill assessment).
**Red flags:** Pay rate opacity. Task availability fluctuates. Some reviewers report long waits between assignments.
**Aaron fit: 8/10.** Strong match. Aaron's PM experience and AI fluency align with evaluation work. Non-developer perspective is valued.

## 2. Scale AI (scale.com)

**What it is:** AI data infrastructure company. Offers RLHF, image annotation, semantic segmentation, 3D point cloud work. Also hires for full-time engineering and operations roles.
**Canadian eligibility:** Unclear from careers page. Some roles are US-based. Contractor positions may be available internationally.
**Application:** Through careers portal. Filter by role type and location.
**Pay:** Contractor roles likely US$20-50/hour for skilled evaluation. Full-time roles CA$80K-150K+ depending on level.
**Work volume:** Large company (valued at $14B+). Steady work for contractors on active projects.
**Time to first paid task:** 2-4 weeks for contractor onboarding. Longer for full-time roles.
**Red flags:** Most contractor work goes through Outlier (their subsidiary). Direct Scale roles tend to be more technical.
**Aaron fit: 6/10.** Better to apply through Outlier for evaluation work. Direct Scale roles may require more technical background.

## 5. Remotasks (remotasks.com)

**What it is:** Crowdsourcing platform for data annotation tasks. 240,000+ workers, 90+ countries. Tasks: transcription, image labelling, LiDAR annotation.
**Canadian eligibility:** Operates in 90+ countries. Canada likely included but not confirmed.
**Application:** Sign up with Facebook/Gmail. Complete training courses. Get enabled for project types.
**Pay:** Low end of the spectrum. Simple tasks likely US$3-8/hour. Complex LiDAR work higher. Paid weekly via PayPal.
**Work volume:** High volume of simple tasks. More complex tasks less available.
**Time to first paid task:** 1-3 days (fast onboarding, training courses are short).
**Red flags:** Very low pay for basic tasks. 2% PayPal transaction fee. Volume-dependent income. Not a career -- it's piece work.
**Aaron fit: 3/10.** Too low-value for Aaron's skill level. The $50/hour minimum makes most Remotasks work unviable. Only worth it as a last-resort proof of "paid AI evaluation experience" on a CV.

## 6. Toloka (toloka.ai)

**Canadian eligibility:** Not explicitly stated. Global operation with enterprise focus.
**Application:** "Talk to an expert" contact form. More enterprise-focused than individual contributor recruitment.
**Pay:** Not disclosed. Enterprise contracts, not individual gig rates.
**Work volume:** Unknown for individual contributors. 70%+ of contributors hold advanced degrees.
**Time to first paid task:** Unknown. Application process appears longer and more selective.
**Red flags:** Primarily a B2B data company, not a gig platform. Individual contributor recruitment unclear.
**Aaron fit: 5/10.** The red-teaming and safety testing angle is interesting, but the entry point is unclear. Better suited if Aaron had a PhD or specific domain certification.
