## Opening Scenario

Vera, 78, has been paying $150/month for her internet for three years. She has a 25 Mbps plan with a 200 GB data cap. Last month, her daughter visited for two weeks and streamed movies every evening. Vera got a $100 overage charge on her next bill. She had no idea what a "data cap" was or that streaming used data. She thought the internet was just "on."

Start from this moment. Then teach.

## CRTC-Sourced Data to Include

- **25 Mbps is sufficient** for most seniors — email, video calls, streaming one show at a time
- **Average Canadian household** uses 585 GB of data per month (most seniors use far less — typically 50–150 GB)
- **Cellular prices declined 16.7%** from 2023 to 2024 — the market is moving in seniors' favour
- **Comparison tools endorsed by CRTC:** PlanHub.ca, WhistleOut Canada — both free, no sign-up required
- **CCTS (Commission for Complaints for Telecom-television Services):** free complaints body if your provider overcharges

## Sections to Build

1. **The Overage Trap** — what happened to Vera, what a data cap is, why providers don't explain this clearly
2. **What Speed Do You Actually Need?** — 25 Mbps vs 100 Mbps vs 1 Gbps. Visual: speedometer metaphor. Most seniors need 25–50 Mbps.
3. **What Is Data and How Do You Use It?** — email (tiny), video call (medium), streaming (large), downloads (large). Visual: bucket metaphor.
4. **Reading Your Internet Bill** — annotated example bill. What each line means. Where the hidden fees are.
5. **How to Compare Plans** — introduce PlanHub.ca and WhistleOut. Step-by-step: enter your postal code, compare, call your provider with the result.
6. **The Phone Call Script** — exact words to say when calling your provider to negotiate: "I've been a loyal customer for X years. I see [competitor] offers [plan] for [price]. Can you match that?"
7. **When to Switch vs When to Negotiate** — decision tree. If your provider won't budge, here's how to switch without losing your email.
8. **Your Rights** — CCTS complaint process. CRTC Internet Code. You are protected.
9. **Quiz** — 5 questions testing key concepts
10. **What You Learned** — summary bullets
