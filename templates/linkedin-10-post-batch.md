## Post 8 — Wednesday April 23 (PROOF: Trust-First Design)

The worst thing a digital product can do to a senior is make them feel stupid.

Every design decision in the Digital Confidence Centre starts with one question: "Would Brenda understand this?"

Brenda is our test persona. She's 74, lives alone in St. Thomas, uses an iPad her daughter gave her for Christmas, and is terrified of clicking the wrong button.

If Brenda can't use it at 150% zoom with shaky hands and no tech vocabulary — we redesign it.

This philosophy — trust-first design — works for seniors. It also works for municipal software. It works for any product where the user didn't choose to be there but has to use it anyway.
