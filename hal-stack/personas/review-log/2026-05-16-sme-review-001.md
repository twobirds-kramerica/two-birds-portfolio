---

### DR. LENA — Child & Digital Development
**Verdict: Approve with 3 annotations**

1. **Activity length:** Four parts in sequence is too long for a 4-6 year old. Reframe as four separate micro-sessions (5-10 min each), ideally spread across a week. Spaced repetition supports habit formation better than one long session.

2. **Learning objective language:** Change from "understands permissions" to "pauses and asks a grown-up before tapping." That is the developmentally achievable and testable outcome at age 4-6.

3. **Core framing is excellent:** "The eye is working / resting" — perfect pre-operational language. "Show your own face" opener — developmentally irresistible for this age group.

---

### FRANK — Frontline Practitioner
**Verdict: Approve with 2 annotations**

1. **Part 4 (smart speaker) = optional.** Many families — especially in DCC's target demographics — do not own a smart speaker. Mark Part 4 explicitly optional: "If your family has a smart speaker..." Library programmes cannot run this part without bringing their own device.

2. **Add materials note.** The camera cover sticker activity requires a physical sticky note or webcam cover. Add one line: "Materials needed: one sticky note or webcam cover per child (~$0.10)." This makes the lesson immediately packageable for library programme coordinators.

**Programme deployability:** High. If Part 4 is optional and materials are listed, this is one of the most deliverable 4-6 modules for a library drop-in setting.
