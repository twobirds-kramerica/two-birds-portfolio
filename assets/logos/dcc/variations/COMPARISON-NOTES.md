# DCC Logo — Variation Comparison Notes

8 variations exploring how to represent "digital confidence" as a warm, welcoming brand.

---

## DCC-V01 — Shield + Checkmark

A rounded shield outline with a bold checkmark inside. Communicates "safe, verified, protected." The shield reads as trust; the checkmark reads as accomplishment.

**What makes it different:** Most direct symbol of safety and verification. Strongest "you're protected here" message.
**Recommended use:** All-purpose. Favicon, site header, print materials. Works everywhere.
**Favicon size?** Excellent — shield and checkmark are both recognisable at 16px.
**Warm and safe?** Safe, yes. Warm, moderately — the shield could feel institutional to some. But the teal background softens it.

## DCC-V02 — Sunrise

A half-circle rising above a horizon line with three rays emanating upward. Subtle ground dots below suggest stepping stones or digital pixels. "New day, new confidence."

**What makes it different:** Only variation with a strong upward/optimistic metaphor. The stepping stones add a learning-journey feel.
**Recommended use:** Site hero images, promotional materials. Slightly complex for favicon.
**Favicon size?** Moderate — the rays and dots may blur at 16px, but the arc reads.
**Warm and safe?** Very warm. A sunrise is universally positive and hopeful.

## DCC-V03 — Open Book + Glow

An open book shape with two curved pages meeting at a spine. A soft glowing circle floats above the book — suggesting the digital screen light illuminating learning.

**What makes it different:** Most literal "learning" symbol. The glow adds the digital element without being techy.
**Recommended use:** Educational contexts. Print materials for library partnerships.
**Favicon size?** Moderate — the book shape is recognisable but the glow detail is lost at very small sizes.
**Warm and safe?** Warm. A book is a trusted, non-threatening object. The glow adds gentle modernity.

## DCC-V04 — Arrow in Circle

A bold upward arrow inside a circle. Clean, confident, unmistakable. "Moving forward, growing upward."

**What makes it different:** Simplest and boldest variation. Most "app icon" feel. Highest clarity at all sizes.
**Recommended use:** Favicon. App icon. Any context where clarity trumps warmth.
**Favicon size?** Excellent — the simplest design in the set. Perfectly legible at 16px.
**Warm and safe?** Confident more than warm. This feels like a fitness app more than a community centre. Strong but perhaps too corporate for DCC's audience.

## DCC-V05 — Bridge

An arch connecting two pillars with a surface line. "Crossing from analog to digital. Bridging the gap."

**What makes it different:** Most narrative variation. The bridge literally represents the journey from "I can't" to "I can."
**Recommended use:** Print. Presentations. Partnership proposals. Tells a clear story to library directors.
**Favicon size?** Good — the arch is recognisable but the pillars may blur.
**Warm and safe?** Warm and purposeful. A bridge is a journey, not a destination — it says "we'll help you get there."

## DCC-V06 — Two Hands Reaching

Two simplified hands reaching toward each other with a connection spark between them. "Support, community, we're in this together."

**What makes it different:** Most human variation. Emphasises the people, not the technology.
**Recommended use:** Community materials. "About us" pages. Volunteer/ambassador recruitment.
**Favicon size?** Poor — the hands are too detailed for 16px. Would need simplification for favicon use.
**Warm and safe?** Warmest in the set. The most "community centre" feel.

## DCC-V07 — Heart + Light Bulb

A light bulb shape with a small heart inside. "Understanding with love. The 'aha' moment, wrapped in care."

**What makes it different:** Most emotionally resonant. Combines understanding (bulb) with care (heart). Memorable silhouette.
**Recommended use:** All-purpose. Strong emotional connection. Good for merchandise, stickers, community materials.
**Favicon size?** Good — the bulb silhouette is recognisable. The heart is very small but the overall shape works.
**Warm and safe?** Very warm. A heart inside a light bulb says "we care about your understanding." This is the most "DCC" feeling logo in the set.

## DCC-V08 — Open Door

A door frame with a door slightly ajar, warm light rays streaming out. A door handle adds a tangible detail. "Welcome in. Come as you are."

**What makes it different:** Most inviting. The open door is a universal symbol of welcome. The light says "something good is inside."
**Recommended use:** Website hero. Welcome page. First-visit experience.
**Favicon size?** Moderate — the door frame reads but the light rays are lost at 16px.
**Warm and safe?** Very warm and inviting. The most "you belong here" variation.
