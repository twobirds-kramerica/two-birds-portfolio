### 6. Secret stuff and share stuff
**Category:** Tech-Safety | **ID:** 349a09cf-876a-8149
**Three annotations (no shared hard stop):**
1. **Vera:** If DCC ever implements child user profiles or signup at any age, the data collected must be consistent with what this module teaches. A child who learns "your name is SECRET STUFF" and then is asked to type their name into DCC's signup form would experience a contradiction. Platform-level check: DCC child-facing features must never collect what this module categorises as secret.
2. **Dr. Lena:** The "home alone" item in SECRET STUFF is correct — but add nuance: "Home alone is secret from strangers and apps — but always, always tell a safe grown-up." This prevents the lesson from inadvertently teaching a child to hide unsafe situations from all adults. Direct link to the Who is my safe grown-up? row — build these consecutively.
3. **Frank:** Some children aged 4-6 do not yet know their home address. The activity should note: "If they don't know it yet, that's fine — the skill is 'don't share it,' not 'memorise it.'" This prevents a teaching moment from becoming an embarrassing gap.
**Verdict: APPROVED — 3 build-spec annotations. Build with Row 7 (Who is my safe grown-up?) as a pair.**

### 7. Who is my safe grown-up?
**Category:** Emotional-Safety | **ID:** 349a09cf-876a-816a
**Vera:** Clear. No privacy exposure. The "safe grown-ups never ask you to keep secrets from other safe grown-ups" rule is gold-standard child protection language.
**Two annotations:**
1. **Dr. Lena:** The activity asks children to name 3-5 safe grown-ups. For children in single-parent households, care arrangements, or with limited family networks, naming 3-5 people may cause anxiety ("I only have 1"). Add explicitly: "Even 1 or 2 safe grown-ups is enough. A teacher, a neighbour, or a school counsellor counts." This is especially important for DCC's outreach to vulnerable families.
2. **Frank:** Library and programme facilitators running this activity may encounter children from disrupted family situations (foster care, domestic violence situations, recent loss). Add a brief facilitator note: "If a child struggles to name people or becomes upset, redirect gently: 'Your teacher or your librarian can be a safe grown-up too.' Do not press." This prepares facilitators without alarming them.
**Verdict: APPROVED — 2 annotations. Build with Row 6 (Secret stuff and share stuff) as a pair.**
