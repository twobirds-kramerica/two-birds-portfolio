## 2. Discovery Call Agenda (30-Minute Format)

**Purpose:** Understand the client's situation, identify the core problem, and determine fit.

**Before the call:**
- Send agenda 24 hours in advance
- Review any materials the client has shared
- Prepare 2–3 open-ended questions based on their industry

---

**[0:00–2:00] Warm-up**
- Thank them for their time
- Brief framing: "My goal today is to understand your situation — I'll ask a lot of questions. There's no pitch."

**[2:00–8:00] Their situation**
- "Tell me about your business and what you're working on right now."
- "What does a good week look like vs a bad week?"

**[8:00–18:00] The problem**
- "What's the one thing that, if it were solved, would make the biggest difference?"
- "How long has this been an issue? What have you tried?"
- "Who else is involved in solving this?"

**[18:00–24:00] Fit check**
- "Here's what I typically do with businesses like yours..." (brief, 2 sentences)
- "Does that resonate with what you're describing?"

**[24:00–28:00] Next steps**
- If there's fit: "I'd recommend we start with an AI Workflow Audit. It's a structured half-day engagement — here's how it works..."
- If no fit: "Based on what you've described, I don't think I'm the right fit right now — but here's who I'd recommend."

**[28:00–30:00] Close**
- Confirm next steps clearly
- Send a follow-up email within 2 hours

## 3. AI Workflow Audit Delivery Checklist

**Engagement:** $2,500 | Half-day (4 hours on-site or virtual)

Use this checklist to prepare and deliver every AI Workflow Audit consistently.

**Pre-audit (day before):**
- [ ] Send pre-work questionnaire to client (5 questions about their current tools and processes)
- [ ] Review their website, LinkedIn, and any materials they've shared
- [ ] Prepare blank audit template (process map + opportunity matrix)
- [ ] Confirm call or on-site time

**During the audit:**
- [ ] Map current workflows: how does work flow from intake to delivery?
- [ ] Identify top 5 time-consuming manual tasks
- [ ] Flag tasks suitable for AI automation (repetitive, rule-based, high-volume)
- [ ] Flag tasks NOT suitable (relationship-dependent, legal, safety-critical)
- [ ] Estimate time savings per task (hours/week)
- [ ] Prioritise by effort vs impact (2x2 matrix)
- [ ] Demo 1–2 specific AI tools relevant to their context
- [ ] Capture questions and concerns — address each one

**Deliverables (within 3 business days):**
- [ ] Audit report (PDF): current state map, opportunity list, priority matrix, recommendations
- [ ] Tool recommendation sheet (3–5 specific tools with rationale)
- [ ] Implementation roadmap (30/60/90 day)
- [ ] Invoice issued

**Follow-up:**
- [ ] 2-week check-in call (30 minutes, no charge)
- [ ] Ask for a written testimonial if outcome was positive
- [ ] Offer retainer proposal if ongoing support is needed

## 4. Retainer Kickoff Agenda (60-Minute Format)

**Use this for:** Clients who have signed a $4,000/month Fractional AI Leadership retainer.

**Objectives:** Align on goals, set up working rhythm, establish communication norms.

---

**[0:00–5:00] Welcome and framing**
- "This is a working relationship, not a consulting engagement. I'm joining your team part-time."
- Confirm scope: X hours/month, what's in and out of scope

**[5:00–20:00] Business situation deep-dive**
- Current state: what are you building, selling, or trying to fix?
- Key initiatives for the next 90 days
- Constraints: budget, team, time, technical

**[20:00–35:00] AI priorities**
- Where are you already using AI? What's working / not working?
- Where are you most curious about AI?
- Where are you most anxious about AI?

**[35:00–50:00] Working rhythm**
- Meeting cadence: weekly check-in (30 min) + monthly strategy review (60 min)
- Async communication: preferred channel (email, Slack, Teams?)
- How do you want me to escalate blockers or decisions?
- Who else on your team should I have a relationship with?

**[50:00–58:00] First deliverable**
- Agree on one concrete output for the first two weeks
- Confirm due date and format

**[58:00–60:00] Close**
- "Any questions before we get started?"
- Send kickoff summary email within 24 hours

## 6. Offboarding Checklist

**Use this when:** A retainer ends (planned or unplanned), or a project-based engagement wraps up.

**Timeline:** Begin 2 weeks before final day.

**Week before final:**
- [ ] Send offboarding notice to client confirming final date
- [ ] Identify all files, documents, and accounts that Aaron has access to
- [ ] Transfer ownership of any shared documents (Google Drive, Notion, etc.)
- [ ] Document any in-progress work with clear status and next steps
- [ ] Hand off any open decisions or blockers to the client or their team

**Final week:**
- [ ] Deliver final report or deliverable (if applicable)
- [ ] Remove Aaron from shared Slack, Teams, or communication channels
- [ ] Revoke access to any client tools, accounts, or systems
- [ ] Send final invoice and confirm payment terms

**After final day:**
- [ ] Send a closing email: thank them, summarise what was accomplished, offer a warm door for future work
- [ ] Request a LinkedIn recommendation (optional, only if outcome was strong)
- [ ] Add to CRM as "Former Client — warm" for future outreach
- [ ] Archive all client files in a clearly labelled folder locally

**If the engagement ends early or on difficult terms:**
- Do not skip the final invoice
- Deliver all promised deliverables even if truncated
- Keep the closing email professional and brief
- Do not discuss the engagement publicly
