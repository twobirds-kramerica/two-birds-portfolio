# Logo v1.2 — Variation Comparison Notes

10 variations exploring spacing, weight, color, and composition fixes for the Two Birds Innovation logo.

---

## V01 — Wide Spread, Bold (Conservative)

Fixes the #1 problem from v1.1: cramped vertical spacing. Chevrons are pushed apart with 124px vertical gap and 56px bold strokes. Open-end circles placed asymmetrically (upper arm on top chevron, lower arm on bottom) for fraternal differentiation.

**Different from others:** The baseline "fix" — same concept as v1.1 but with breathing room and confidence.
**Best for:** All-purpose. Works at favicon through social media.
**Commands strength?** Yes — the bold strokes and generous spacing project confidence without shouting.
**Balanced?** Yes — mathematically centred at (512, 512).

## V02 — Maximum Canvas, Extra Bold (Conservative)

Pushes the chevrons to fill the maximum usable area. 64px strokes — the heaviest in the set. Every pixel of canvas padding is used. This is the "I see it from across the room" version.

**Different from others:** Largest footprint, heaviest weight. Most aggressive canvas usage.
**Best for:** Social media avatars, app icons where the logo appears small. LinkedIn profile picture.
**Commands strength?** Strongest in the set. Almost aggressive.
**Balanced?** Yes, but the heavy weight makes it feel slightly imposing rather than inviting.

## V03 — Symmetric Elegant (Conservative)

Equal horizontal offset from centre for both chevrons. Clean 50px strokes. Fraternal difference is subtle: top circle is 28px, bottom is 22px — you notice it on second look. The most refined variation.

**Different from others:** Perfect geometric symmetry. The "luxury brand" feel.
**Best for:** Print. Business cards. Letterhead. Situations where elegance matters more than impact.
**Commands strength?** Moderate — elegant rather than powerful. Might feel delicate at small sizes.
**Balanced?** Perfectly balanced by design.

## V04 — Fraternal Stroke Weights (Conservative)

Same geometry as V01 but the fraternal difference is in the stroke weight: top chevron is 56px (bolder), bottom is 42px (lighter). The heavier chevron feels protective; the lighter one feels agile. No open-end circles — the weight difference IS the differentiator.

**Different from others:** Only variation where the chevrons are literally different weights. Most visible fraternal distinction.
**Best for:** All-purpose. The weight difference reads clearly at every size.
**Commands strength?** Yes — the heavy top chevron anchors the composition. The lighter bottom adds movement.
**Balanced?** Visually balanced despite asymmetric weights — the heavier top is offset by the lighter bottom.

## V05 — Cosmos Texture, Large Circles (Conservative)

Retains the cosmos texture from v1.1 but with better spacing and larger identity circles (34px). The texture adds depth and personality — "universe" feel. The larger circles make the identity markers more visible at small sizes.

**Different from others:** Only conservative variation with cosmos texture. Largest circles (34px).
**Best for:** Digital-first contexts. Social media banners. Presentation slides. Not ideal for print (texture won't reproduce well in CMYK).
**Commands strength?** Moderate — the texture softens the impact slightly. More personality than power.
**Balanced?** Yes. The texture fills negative space evenly.

## V06 — Dark Navy, Warm/Cool Whites (Moderate)

Deep navy background (#0D1B2A — almost black) with warm cream top chevron (#FFF8E1) and cool ice bottom chevron (#E3F2FD). The fraternal difference is temperature: one is warm-toned, the other cool-toned. Subtle but effective — you feel the difference before you see it.

**Different from others:** Only variation with a dark background. Only one using color temperature for fraternal distinction.
**Best for:** Premium contexts. Dark-mode interfaces. Evening presentations. Print on dark stock.
**Commands strength?** Yes — the dark background creates gravitas. The warm/cool split adds sophistication.
**Balanced?** Balanced. The color temperature difference is subtle enough not to create visual weight imbalance.

## V07 — Gradient Background, Size-Different Circles (Moderate)

Blue gradient background (darker at bottom) with dramatically different circle sizes: 36px on the top chevron, 20px on the bottom. The gradient adds subtle depth. The circle size difference is the most obvious fraternal marker in the set.

**Different from others:** Only variation with a gradient. Most dramatic circle size difference.
**Best for:** Digital. App icons. Presentations. The gradient won't print well in offset.
**Commands strength?** Yes — the gradient adds dimensionality. The large top circle is a strong anchor point.
**Balanced?** The circle size difference creates intentional visual asymmetry — balanced in tension, not in symmetry.

## V08 — Asymmetric Angles (Moderate)

The top chevron has a tight 200px arm spread (compact, focused). The bottom has a wide 360px spread (expansive, open). Same stroke weight, same color — the shape itself carries the fraternal difference. One daughter is focused; the other is expansive. Different energy, same family.

**Different from others:** Only variation where the chevrons have genuinely different shapes. Most structural fraternal distinction.
**Best for:** All-purpose. The shape difference reads at every size. Strong storytelling.
**Commands strength?** Yes — the wide bottom chevron anchors the composition with visual weight. The tight top feels decisive.
**Balanced?** Deliberately imbalanced for narrative effect. The wider bottom grounds the lighter top.

## V09 — Overlapping Zone (Creative)

The chevrons share vertical space — their arms weave through the same region of the canvas. The top chevron is slightly translucent (85% opacity), creating a layered effect where you see both chevrons simultaneously in the overlap zone. Represents connection, shared space, intertwined paths.

**Different from others:** Only variation where chevrons overlap. Only one using transparency. Most dynamic composition.
**Best for:** Digital hero images. Large format. Conference slides. Not suitable for favicon (too complex at small sizes).
**Commands strength?** Different kind of strength — interconnection rather than dominance. Feels alive and dynamic.
**Balanced?** The overlap creates visual tension that's compelling but might feel busy at small sizes.

## V10 — Big + Small (Creative)

One large chevron (60px stroke, fills the upper-left) and one smaller chevron (40px stroke, tucked into the lower-right). The scale difference is dramatic — the large one is roughly 2x the small one. Represents a protective relationship: one bird sheltering the other. Or a parent and child. Or a mentor and student.

**Different from others:** Only variation with dramatically different scales. Most narrative variation. Breaks the "two equal halves" assumption.
**Best for:** Storytelling contexts. Brand presentations. "About us" pages. Not ideal for favicon (small chevron disappears).
**Commands strength?** The large chevron commands enormous strength. The small one adds vulnerability and tenderness.
**Balanced?** Deliberately unbalanced — the asymmetry IS the point. Works because both elements share the same visual language.
