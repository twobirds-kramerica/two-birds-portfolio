# Brain Trustee Review Matrix — Two Birds Innovation

**Status:** v1.0 · **Created:** 2026-05-10 · **Sprint:** S-FOUNDING-BOARD

> The Brain Trust is the group you convene when a decision is high-stakes enough
> to warrant more than one perspective. This matrix routes decision types to the
> right combination of advisors and departments so you convene the right room —
> not the largest one.
>
> Source concept: Ed Catmull, *Creativity Inc.* — the Pixar Brain Trust as a
> model for structured peer review without authority transfer. The Brain Trust
> advises. Aaron decides.

---

## How to Use This Matrix

1. Identify the decision type from the left column.
2. See which Founding Board members and operational departments to convene.
3. Invoke them in order: Founding Board advisors first, then operational departments, then The Hand synthesizes.
4. Aaron decides. Brain Trust advises. Authority never transfers.

## Convening Size Guidelines

| Stakes Level | Recommended Size | Rationale |
|-------------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Low** — reversible, low cost | Scrappy Pack only (5 advisors) | Fast, directional, no deep analysis needed |
| **Medium** — meaningful but recoverable | 1-2 Founding Board + 1-2 departments | Targeted expertise, not a committee |
| **High** — hard to reverse, significant cost | 3-4 Founding Board + 2-3 departments | Full debate, higher confidence |
| **Critical** — pivot, major hire, raise, major client | All 6 Founding Board + all relevant departments | Full board, The Hand synthesizes last |

**Avoid:** Convening the full board for a medium decision. Committee bloat slows decisions without improving them. Match room size to stakes.

## Brain Trust Protocol

1. **State the decision clearly.** One sentence. "I am deciding whether to [X]."
2. **Provide context.** What you know, what you don't, what the options are, what the constraints are.
3. **Name the trust.** "Run this through the [decision type] Brain Trust."
4. **Advisors respond in matrix order.** Founding Board first, then operational departments.
5. **The Hand synthesizes.** Trade-offs, recommendation, confidence level.
6. **Aaron decides.** The Brain Trust advises. Authority never transfers.
7. **Log the decision.** One line in `hal-stack/architecture/decisions/` if significant.

---

## What the Brain Trust Is Not

- **Not a permission system.** Aaron doesn't need the board's approval. He seeks their perspective.
- **Not a way to delay decisions.** If the Brain Trust can't resolve it in one session, The Hand calls the decision with current information.
- **Not always right.** Advisors can be wrong. Aaron can override any advisor with reasoning.
- **Not a replacement for talking to real customers.** Sonia will always remind you: go talk to three real users before any major product decision.
