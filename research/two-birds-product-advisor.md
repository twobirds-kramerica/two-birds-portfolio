## Section H — Persona Builder Strategic Note

### What Is the Persona Builder Concept?
The Persona Builder is a shared component across DCC, Career Coach, and Clarity that creates a user profile through progressive disclosure:
- **DCC:** Brenda persona (74, iPad, St. Thomas) — used as a design lens
- **Career Coach:** Onboarding captures name, employment status, priorities, CV
- **Clarity:** Captures business name, industry, team size, challenges, AI usage, revenue goal

The concept: a **universal persona engine** that builds a rich user profile across all Three products, enabling personalised experiences and cross-product recommendations.

### How It Connects the Three Products
- A user takes the Clarity diagnostic → their business profile feeds into Career Coach if they're also job searching → their digital literacy level from DCC informs how Clarity presents its results
- A library that white-labels DCC could also offer Career Coach to job-seeking patrons, with the persona carrying across
- A municipality using Paperwork Labs + DCC could pre-populate citizen profiles from form data

### Is It Worth Building Now?
**No.** The Persona Builder is a product vision, not a revenue driver. It requires:
- Cross-product data sharing (complex)
- Privacy framework for shared profiles (legal review needed)
- All three products to have paying users (currently zero)
- Technical integration work (localStorage sync, potential backend)

**Build it when:** At least 2 of the 3 products have paying customers and there's a concrete use case from a real client who would benefit from cross-product profiles.

### What Would It Unlock Commercially?
If built well, the Persona Builder creates a **platform play** rather than a portfolio of individual tools. The pitch becomes: "We don't just offer a literacy platform and a career tool — we offer a connected ecosystem that follows the citizen from learning to employment to business building." This is a much bigger story for enterprise sales, grants, and potential acquisition interest.

**But that's a 2027 conversation, not a 2026 one.**
