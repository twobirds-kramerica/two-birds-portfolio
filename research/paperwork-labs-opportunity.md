## Section 1 — Company Profile

### What Paperwork Labs Does
[Paperwork](https://www.paperworkforms.com/) is a digital forms platform that replaces PDF downloads and paper forms with fully web-based, mobile-responsive form submissions. Their value proposition: no complicated downloading, no PDFs, no Adobe Reader. A form goes on your website as a simple link, and you choose who receives submissions.

The platform handles public-facing forms, internal staff paperwork, vendor interactions, and exchanges with other stakeholders. This is foundational infrastructure for municipal digital services.

### Market Position
- **Stage:** Early growth. Small team (likely 5–15 based on product scope and St. Thomas base).
- **Customers:** Municipal governments, likely starting with Ontario small-to-mid municipalities.
- **Funding:** Unknown — no public funding rounds found. Likely bootstrapped or seed-stage.
- **Revenue estimate:** CA$200k–$1M ARR based on platform pricing and municipal contract size. This is speculative.

### AI Gaps (The Opportunity)
Paperwork's current product is form management — essentially digitising paper processes. The AI gap is significant:
- No intelligent form routing (AI reads submission, assigns to correct department)
- No auto-population from prior submissions
- No natural language form completion (speak your answer, AI fills the form)
- No analytics dashboard predicting form volume or processing bottlenecks
- No compliance checking (AI validates submissions against bylaws/regulations)

These are all high-value additions that a fractional AI leader could roadmap and execute.

### Civic Partnership History
St. Thomas has active economic development partnerships including the Small Business Enterprise Centre (SBEC), TechAlliance London, and Elgin Business Resource Centre. The StaySafe Elgin initiative demonstrates the region's appetite for civic tech solutions.

## Section 2 — Market Opportunity

### Ontario Municipal TAM
Ontario has 444 municipalities. Most are still using PDFs, email attachments, or paper for citizen-facing forms. The market for municipal form digitisation is large and underpenetrated.

Ontario's government has published a [Trustworthy AI Framework](https://www.ontario.ca/page/ontarios-trustworthy-artificial-intelligence-ai-framework) and [Building Digital Ontario](https://www.ontario.ca/page/building-digital-ontario) strategy, signalling top-down pressure for municipalities to modernise. The [Government Innovation Showcase Ontario 2026](https://publicsectornetwork.com/events/government-innovation-showcase-ontario-toronto/) demonstrates growing appetite.

A [University of Toronto study on AI governance in municipalities](https://schoolofcities.utoronto.ca/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Building-AI-Governance-in-Municipalities-from-the-Ground-Up.pdf) (February 2026) confirms this is an active, policy-relevant space.

### VW/PowerCo Effect on St. Thomas
The $7 billion PowerCo electric vehicle battery plant is transforming St. Thomas:
- **3,000 direct jobs** at the plant + **30,000 indirect jobs** regionally
- **$130 million in new assessment growth** for 2025 alone
- **$400 million in water infrastructure** upgrades needed
- Construction has begun, with production set for 2027

**The permit volume effect:** Every one of those 30,000+ new workers needs housing. Housing needs permits. Permits need forms. Inspections need forms. Business licences need forms. The municipal form volume in Elgin County is about to surge dramatically. Paperwork Labs is positioned exactly where this demand will land.

Paperwork's advantage: local, focused on Canadian municipalities, lightweight product, lower price point. Disadvantage: no AI capability and likely underfunded compared to US-backed competitors.

## Section 7 — Risks and Honest Assessment

### Risks for Aaron
1. **Paperwork Labs may not be able to pay market rate.** If they're pre-revenue or early revenue, expect CA$4,000–$6,000/month + equity, not CA$18,000+. Aaron must clarify funding and compensation range in the first conversation.
2. **Equity in an early-stage govtech startup is illiquid.** Don't count it as income. It's a lottery ticket.
3. **3 days/week may creep to 5.** Startups always need more. Aaron must protect his Two Birds days contractually.
4. **Opportunity cost.** Every day at Paperwork Labs is a day not spent on DCC white-label sales or consulting clients. If DCC gets a CA$15k contract, the math changes.
5. **Dependency on one income source.** If Paperwork Labs runs out of money or pivots, Aaron is back to zero.

### Risks for Paperwork Labs
1. **Aaron has no track record specifically in govtech.** His Telus experience is adjacent but not identical.
2. **Split attention.** Two Birds will always be in the background. Some founders resent this.
3. **Advisory councils are expensive.** 4 advisors + Aaron = CA$15,000–$25,000/month in advisory costs. For a startup, this is significant.

### Questions Aaron Must Ask Before Committing
1. What is your current monthly revenue and burn rate?
2. How many paying municipal customers do you have?
3. What is your runway (months of cash remaining)?
4. Have you raised outside funding, and do you plan to?
5. What does the AI roadmap look like today — is this greenfield or are there existing efforts?
6. Who would I report to, and who has final say on product decisions?
7. What is the budget for the advisory council, and is it approved?
8. What happens if funding doesn't come through — is this role at risk?

### Red Flags to Watch For
- They want full-time commitment disguised as "fractional"
- No clear budget for AI investment beyond Aaron's time
- Founder resistance to external advisors (wants to keep control)
- No paying customers yet (form management is a solved problem — if they haven't sold it by now, ask why)
- Equity offered instead of cash with vague vesting terms

### Is This the Right Move for $10k/month by August?

**Maybe, but only if the cash component is CA$6,000+/month.** If Paperwork Labs offers CA$4,000/month + equity, Aaron needs two additional consulting clients to bridge to CA$10k. That's tight but possible.

The safer path to $10k/month is: 2 consulting audits (CA$5,000) + 1 retainer client (CA$4,000) + DCC white-label pilot (CA$1,500/month) = CA$10,500. This path doesn't depend on any single company's ability to pay.

**Verdict:** Pursue Paperwork Labs, but in parallel with consulting pipeline. Do not put all eggs in one basket.
