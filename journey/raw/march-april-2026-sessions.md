# Raw Session Log — March–April 2026

---

## March 25-28, 2026 — The 72-Hour Sprint

Built 7 digital products across 4 days. The Digital Confidence Centre came first — 241 pages, 21 modules, bilingual English and French, WCAG 2.1 AA accessible. A complete digital literacy curriculum for Canadian seniors, from scratch, in a weekend.

Career Coach followed — an AI-powered job application tracker that helps people organise and improve their applications. Then Clarity — a diagnostic tool that gives Ontario SMEs a SWOT analysis and action plan in 60 seconds. Kevin's Apartment Search — a personalised apartment hunting dashboard with commute scoring. Quality Dashboard for internal monitoring.

Worked past midnight multiple nights. Switched between the i5 Lenovo and the Pentium Silver mid-build when the i5 needed charging or got too hot. The Pentium was painfully slow but kept the momentum alive. Gmail drafts became the external memory system out of necessity — when context windows filled up, Aaron would dictate summaries into Gmail and paste them into the next session.

The pace was unsustainable and everyone involved knew it. But the output was real. Seven working products, all deployed, all functional. The kind of sprint that proves something to yourself.

## March 29, 2026 — Voice Notes and Meeting Prep

Three voice dictation sessions to prepare the Mike Kerkvliet meeting brief. Aaron walked around the house talking into his phone, capturing thoughts about Two Birds positioning, the VW/PowerCo opportunity, the consulting pricing model.

Context rot fear was real — the worry that everything built in the sprint would fade from memory if not documented immediately. This led to the creation of portable summary documents: SESSION-STATE.md, NEXT-SPRINT-QUEUE.md, the WIP dashboard. Infrastructure born from anxiety.

First time the Brenda interview data shaped a real product decision. The composite persona of a 74-year-old senior afraid of her iPad became the lens through which every DCC design choice was validated. "Would Brenda understand this?" became the test.

## March 30, 2026 — The Meetings

Coffee with Mike Kerkvliet at Streamliners, 9am. St. Thomas. Two experienced professionals talking honestly about what comes next. Not a pitch — a peer conversation. Two significant opportunities floated during the meeting, both exploratory, both worth following up.

Phil Butler meeting prep completed in the afternoon. CV rewritten in a human voice — not the corporate template, but the real story. Twenty years of product leadership distilled into something that sounds like a person, not a LinkedIn profile.

The meetings felt like a turning point. After days of building in isolation, talking to other humans about the work made it feel tangible. The products existed. The consulting model had a shape. The revenue path had names attached to it.

GitHub Actions inbox flooding fixed — the workflows were firing on every push and generating dozens of email notifications. Turned off the noise. Clarity built and pushed to GitHub. Overnight scheduler created for the first time. HAL Stack Phase 1 foundation completed — the architecture document, setup guide, roadmap, prompt tracking schema.

Five Claude Code sessions in a single day. Worked until nearly midnight. The "just one more thing" energy that comes from momentum — each completed task revealing the next obvious task, the queue never shrinking, the progress never feeling quite finished.
