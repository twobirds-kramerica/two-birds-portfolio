## Opening Scenario

Margaret, 76, pays $220/month for a bundle: internet, cable TV with 300 channels, and a home phone. She watches 4 channels. She uses her home phone to call her sister once a week. She has never questioned the bill because "that's just what it costs." Her granddaughter showed her how to watch the same shows on her iPad for free with an antenna and a streaming app. Margaret was upset — not at the cost, but that nobody told her sooner.

Start from this moment. Then teach.

## Sections to Build

1. **Margaret's Bundle** — the anatomy of a triple-play bundle. What each part costs individually vs bundled. Why bundles exist (to keep you paying for things you don't use).
2. **Do You Still Need Cable TV?** — honest assessment. If you watch fewer than 10 channels, you likely don't. Alternatives: digital antenna (free local channels), one streaming service ($7–15/month), library streaming (free with library card).
3. **Do You Still Need a Home Phone?** — if you have a mobile phone, probably not. If you need a landline for medical alert systems, check if the system works with VoIP or mobile.
4. **Streaming for Beginners** — what streaming is, how it works on your iPad/TV. One service is enough. Help them pick: what do you watch? News = CBC Gem (free). Movies = one paid service. Sports = specific guidance.
5. **The Digital Antenna** — what it is, how it works (plug in, scan channels), what you get (CBC, CTV, Global, CityTV — free, no monthly cost). Where to buy ($25–40 at any electronics store).
6. **Reading Your Bundle Bill** — annotated example. Show the true cost of each component. Highlight: "You are paying $X for channels you never watch."
7. **How to Unbundle** — step-by-step: call provider, ask to remove cable TV, keep internet. Expected savings. What to say if they offer a "retention deal."
8. **Your Rights** — CCTS covers TV and phone complaints too. Right to cancel. No cancellation fees on month-to-month plans.
9. **Quiz** — 5 questions
10. **What You Learned** — summary bullets
